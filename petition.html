Grant Munashe Tyrone His Rightful Identity and Heritage

We call upon the global community and the South African Government, particularly the Department of Home Affairs, to address the urgent plight of Munashe Tyrone. As the son of Trevor Tutu and the grandson of the revered human rights activist Desmond Tutu, Munashe was born in Pretoria, South Africa. Despite his grandfather’s celebrated legacy of advocating for human dignity and rights, Munashe faces severe legal and health challenges stemming from his father's omission on his birth certificate. This oversight not only strips him of his rightful heritage but also blocks him from accessing essential services and rights as a South African citizen.

The Issue:

This denial of Munashe’s identity contravenes the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child (Article 8), the South African Constitution regarding human rights and the rights of children, and the very principles that Desmond Tutu dedicated his life to defend. It presents a stark contradiction to the legacy of a man who fought tirelessly for the underserved and marginalized. Now, Munashe must engage in his own battle to secure the basic rights and identity that should be his by birthright.

The Issue:

Munashe Tyrone, has been denied his rightful identity due to the absence of his father's name, Trevor Tutu, on his birth certificate. Despite conclusive DNA evidence confirming paternity, the necessary legal and familial acknowledgment remains withheld, leaving Munashe without the identity and benefits he rightfully deserves.

Munashe's plight is not just a personal issue; it is a glaring misstep in justice that contradicts the very principles South Africa stands for—equality, dignity, and the right to identity. His mother, Dr. Auxilia Goromonzi, has been single-handedly managing the emotional and financial burden of supporting Munashe. This includes covering his medical bills, legal fees, and everyday living expenses, as they fight a painstaking battle for recognition in courts that seem indifferent to their urgency.

Why It Matters:

Munashe's story is a glaring contradiction to the legacy of his grandfather, Desmond Tutu, who tirelessly fought for the rights and dignity of others. It is time for the institutions that Desmond Tutu defended and strengthened to stand up for his grandson, ensuring justice and human rights are universally applied.


Every moment of delay exacerbates the financial and emotional toll on Munashe’s family—particularly his mother, Dr. Auxilia Goromonzi, who has exhausted all resources in her relentless pursuit of justice for her son. This situation demands immediate rectification to uphold the principles of justice and human rights for all.

How You Can Help:

Sign this petition to demand that the South African government take immediate action. By adding your voice, you are advocating for Munashe's rights and supporting the principles of justice and human rights for all. Share this petition with others to amplify our call for action.

Closing:

Thank you for your support. Together, we can ensure that Munashe receives the recognition he deserves and that no other child has to endure such an unjust denial of their rights.
